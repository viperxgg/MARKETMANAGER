# Compliance Rules

These rules apply to lead research, outreach, email campaigns, social content, and visual creative.

## Language Rules

Do not use language that:

- Attacks or embarrasses a company.
- Says or implies that their current website is bad.
- Uses fear, pressure, or shame.
- Promises guaranteed sales, bookings, or revenue.
- Claims results that are not proven.

Use language that:

- Presents an opportunity.
- Respects the company's current position.
- Focuses on customer experience.
- Uses calm Swedish business tone.
- Makes soft calls to action.

Preferred style:

```text
Vi såg en möjlighet att göra kundresan ännu enklare och mer modern för era gäster.
```

Avoid:

```text
Er hemsida är dålig och ni förlorar kunder.
```

## GDPR-Safe Outreach Basics

- Use official business contact details when possible.
- Keep a clear business reason for contact.
- Personalize messages to the recipient company.
- Avoid bulk sending without controls.
- Include a clear opt-out line when appropriate.
- Store source URLs for contact details.
- Do not collect unnecessary personal data.

## Email Compliance Checklist

- The company is relevant to the product.
- The email address came from an official or trustworthy source.
- The message is personalized.
- The message states a legitimate business reason.
- The CTA is soft and low-pressure.
- Unsupported claims are removed.
- Opt-out text is included when appropriate.
- Status is not beyond `owner_review` unless owner-approved.

## Social Compliance Checklist

- No exaggerated claims.
- No fake proof, fake customer stories, or fake screenshots.
- No misleading before/after visuals.
- No aggressive sales pressure.
- The post matches the approved product facts.
- Image prompt does not imply unrealistic outcomes.
